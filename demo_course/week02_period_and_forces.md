# Week 2 Lecture Notes: Period and Restoring Force

For small angles, pendulum period depends mostly on string length and gravitational acceleration. Mass does not appear in ideal period formula, which is why swapping heavier bob does not automatically slow motion.

Restoring force acts along arc toward equilibrium position. As angle grows larger, small-angle approximation becomes less accurate and measured period drifts upward.

Lab reminder: keep launch angle below fifteen degrees when comparing trials. If one team starts from much larger angle, period comparison becomes noisy even before damping is discussed.
